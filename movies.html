MOVIES:

1. Argylle

The greater the spy the bigger the lie? This movie's advertising is the biggest lie of all

Argylle is a spy thriller movie that takes place in the Kingsverse, with other titles such as Kingsmen and the Kingsman. It features Bryce Dallas Howard and Sam Rockwell as the main characters with Bryan Cranston and Catherine O'Hara as the main antagonists, as well as small guest appearances by Samauel L Jackson and Dua Lipa.

...It also has about 5 mins of Henry Cavill and John Cena, even though they were the only reasons I saw the movie, and were made out in the trailer as key characters. Also look at the poster. Henry Cavill is at the very front, despite barely being in it, and has higher billing over both of the main characters... criminal behaviour

Regardless, the movie was fine, although a bit long, and had some good plot twists paired with some very predictable plot twists. The dude I was sitting next to fell asleep midway through the movie, which was probably the funniest thing that happened all night. This movie is supposed to be part comedy btw

Bryan Cranston in the only actor in this movie who can actually act, and Samauel Jackson needs to stop getting involved in these trash movies like this and The Marvels

Finally, Flynn said this was 'the worst movie he had ever seen in theaters', which is a crazy take because we've seen The Marvels and the FNAF movie. THOSE movies are bad


All in all, This movie isn't great but if you have nothing else to do it's worth watching . It's not THAAAAT bad

RATING: 6/10

2. The Beekeeper

To bee or not to bee

This movie is awesome

Beekeeper is a John Wick ripoff featuring Jason Statham, but basically instead of his dog dying his beehives are destroyed. After this random old lady gets scammed, 'Mr. Clay' decides to he has to something something protect the hive something something smoke out the hornets and kill a bunch of people idk doesn't matter

After a lengthy introductory scene of Jason Statham making honey and taking care of bees and shit, the rest of the movie is a non-stop action setpiece of 'The Beekeeper' mowing down hundreds of goons and blowing shit up. When I thought the movie was nearly over and the story was starting to wrap up, it just kept going forever and forever. Despite being only 1hr 45 mins long, it felt about 4

Especially noteworthy about this movie is the great visual effects, which are rare in recent times: see above - Argylle. Some swat dude gets cut in half by a steel wire, Statham ties up a goon to a car and drives it off a bridge, good fun. It definitely earns it's MA15+ rating

To conclude, I loved this movie when it was called transporter, I loved this move when it was called The Expendables, SAFE, etc etc. A must watch.

RATING: 8/10
